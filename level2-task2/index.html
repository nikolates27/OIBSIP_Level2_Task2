Biography

Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Croatia. He was an inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Tesla emigrated to the United States in 1884, where he worked for Thomas Edison before eventually setting out on his own. His inventions and theoretical work laid the groundwork for many modern technologies, including the radio, wireless communication, and the induction motor.

Achievements

  • Developed the AC electrical system
  • Invented the Tesla coil
  • Contributed to the development of radio technology
  • Proposed the concept of wireless communication
  • Designed the first hydroelectric power plant
